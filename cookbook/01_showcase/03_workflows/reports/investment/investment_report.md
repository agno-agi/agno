# Investment Portfolio Report

## Allocation Strategy
Given the rankings and analysis, the allocation strategy is based on the core-satellite approach: 
- JPMorgan Chase & Co. (JPM) will form a core position at 45% of the portfolio.
- Bank of America Corporation (BAC) will be an additional core holding with 35%.
- The Goldman Sachs Group, Inc. (GS) retains a satellite position at 20% given its growth potential tempered by higher risks.

## Investment Thesis
JPMorgan Chase (JPM) is prioritized due to its diversified financial services, stability, and technological advancements which position it well for consistent growth, making it a solid core investment. Bank of America (BAC) complements with strengths in consumer banking and wealth management, offering both value and income potential, especially attractive as economic conditions recover. Goldman Sachs (GS) is included for its high-return potential in positive market conditions but with cautious weighting due to its volatility and regulatory exposure risks.

## Risk Management
For JPM, the strategy focuses on leveraging diversification across products and geographies, maintaining strong capital buffers, and risk assessments to manage regulatory pressures and global economic challenges. For BAC, the focus is on enhancing digital banking and operational efficiency to navigate competitive pressures and macroeconomic changes. For GS, risk management hones in on diversifying revenue streams beyond capital markets and strengthening compliance frameworks to mitigate volatility impacts.

## Final Recommendations
Hold 45% in JPM to capitalize on its comprehensive growth capabilities and stability. Allocate 35% to BAC to benefit from its resilience in consumer banking and economic potential, plus its attractive income proposition. Retain 20% in GS for upside in strong markets while monitoring to respond rapidly to any increase in market volatility or regulatory changes. Monitor economic indicators closely and reassess sector exposures quarterly to ensure alignment with macroeconomic and market developments.
