# Comprehensive Learning Machine Use Cases

This folder contains comprehensive cookbooks that test **all 5 learning stores** in realistic, complex scenarios.

## What Makes These "Comprehensive"?

Unlike focused patterns (07_patterns), these cookbooks:
- ‚úÖ Use **ALL 5 stores** (UserProfile, UserMemory, SessionContext, EntityMemory, LearnedKnowledge)
- ‚úÖ Span **weeks or months** (temporal evolution)
- ‚úÖ Show **emergent intelligence** (stores influencing each other)
- ‚úÖ Feature **realistic complexity** (interruptions, pivots, learning from failures)
- ‚úÖ Demonstrate **clear ROI** (measurable improvements over time)

## Use Case Ideas

### üöÄ **1. Startup Fundraising Assistant** (HIGH PRIORITY)

**Why Compelling:**
- Emotional journey: rejection ‚Üí learning ‚Üí success
- Clear success metrics (raised $X in Y months)
- Pattern recognition crucial (what resonates with VCs)
- Multi-stakeholder (founders, investors, advisors)

**All 5 Stores:**
- **UserProfile**: Founder background (technical/business), fundraising experience
- **UserMemory**: Communication style, stress patterns, decision preferences
- **SessionContext**: Current fundraising round progress (seed, Series A)
- **EntityMemory**: Investors, pitch versions, feedback, warm introductions
- **LearnedKnowledge**: "VCs focusing on AI ask about data moats", "Demo-first pitches convert 40% better"

**Journey:**
1. **Week 1**: Target investor research, build pipeline
2. **Week 3**: First pitches, track objections
3. **Week 6**: Pivot pitch based on feedback patterns
4. **Week 10**: Term sheet negotiations
5. **Week 12**: Close round, save learnings for Series A

**Success Pattern:**
- Initial: 20 pitches ‚Üí 2 second meetings ‚Üí 0 term sheets
- After learning: 15 pitches ‚Üí 8 second meetings ‚Üí 3 term sheets ‚Üí 1 close

### üíº **2. Investment Research Analyst** (HIGH PRIORITY)

**Why Compelling:**
- High stakes (real money decisions)
- Complex entity networks (companies, executives, competitors)
- Thesis evolution over time
- Clear success/failure (portfolio returns)

**All 5 Stores:**
- **UserProfile**: Investment style (value/growth), sector expertise (fintech)
- **UserMemory**: Risk tolerance, research preferences, conviction patterns
- **SessionContext**: Current thesis development (e.g., "vertical SaaS opportunity")
- **EntityMemory**: Companies, executives, earnings calls, market events
- **LearnedKnowledge**: "SaaS companies with >120% NRR outperform by 30%", "Avoid companies in regulatory crosshairs"

**Journey:**
1. **Month 1**: Thesis development - vertical SaaS in healthcare
2. **Month 2**: Deep dive on Company A, interview management
3. **Month 3**: Company A stumbles, pattern recognition from past mistakes
4. **Month 4**: Pivot to Company B with better metrics
5. **Month 6**: Investment pays off, save winning pattern

**Learnings:**
- "Revenue quality > growth rate for healthcare SaaS"
- "Management teams with domain expertise (former doctors) execute better"
- "Regulatory moats in healthcare = competitive advantage"

### üìä **3. Customer Success Manager**

**Why Compelling:**
- Proactive intervention (predict churn before it happens)
- Health score evolution
- Pattern: small signals ‚Üí big outcomes
- B2B relationship complexity

**All 5 Stores:**
- **UserProfile**: CSM style (technical/relationship), industry focus
- **UserMemory**: Communication preferences, escalation patterns
- **SessionContext**: Current account reviews (Q4 renewals)
- **EntityMemory**: Customers, contacts, health scores, usage data, interventions
- **LearnedKnowledge**: "Drop in login frequency 30 days pre-renewal = 60% churn risk", "Executive engagement in Month 2 = 90% retention"

**Journey:**
1. **Q1**: Onboard new customers, baseline health scores
2. **Q2**: Early warning - Customer A login drops, proactive intervention
3. **Q3**: Customer B asks about competitor, save intervention pattern
4. **Q4**: Renewals - apply learned patterns, achieve 95% retention
5. **Next Q1**: New customers benefit from past learnings

**Key Patterns:**
- Churn signals: decreased usage, support ticket spike, C-suite disengagement
- Save interventions: executive QBR, success plan creation, ROI analysis
- Expansion signals: power user emergence, API usage growth, cross-team adoption

### üìö **5. Academic Paper Writing Assistant**

**Why Compelling:**
- Citation networks (complex entity relationships)

- Publication strategy evolution

**All 5 Stores:**
- **UserProfile**: Research area (NLP), writing style, career stage (PhD student)

3. **Year 3**: Paper accepted, save successful patterns

**Success Metrics:**
- Acceptance rate: 20% (Year 1) ‚Üí 60% (Year 3)
- Venue quality: Workshop (Year 1) ‚Üí Top-tier conference (Year 3)
- Citation impact: Learned which framing attracts citations

## Implementation Checklist

For each comprehensive cookbook:
- [ ] Spans 4+ weeks/months (temporal depth)
- [ ] Uses all 5 stores naturally (not forced)
- [ ] Shows store synergy (UserProfile influences EntityMemory, etc.)
- [ ] Has clear success metrics (fundraise closed, portfolio return, churn prevented)
- [ ] Includes failures/pivots (learning from mistakes)
- [ ] Demonstrates pattern recognition (what works, what doesn't)
- [ ] Verification section shows all 5 stores populated
- [ ] Follows established cookbook format (60-char dividers, clean comments)
